# Reading Page｜Madeleine

## Entrance Question

Is memory awakened, or is it constructed under the pressure of signs?

## Peripheral Sources

- Gilles Deleuze, Proust and Signs
- Gérard Genette, Narrative Discourse
- Joshua Landy, Philosophy as Fiction

## Toward Proust

The madeleine scene can be read as an encounter with a sign before it becomes knowledge. Taste does not simply recover the past; it places the narrator under pressure to interpret what has appeared.

## Key Scene

The taste of tea and madeleine opens a passage from sensation to memory, from bodily shock to the reconstruction of Combray.

## Reading Notes

- Sensation arrives before explanation.
- The sign demands interpretation.
- Memory becomes a formed event rather than a stored object.
