# Minimal Entry Packet V0.1

## 1. Text, Time, and Signs

### Peripheral Sources

- Gilles Deleuze, Proust and Signs
- Gérard Genette, Narrative Discourse
- Joshua Landy, Philosophy as Fiction
- Roger Shattuck, Proust's Way

### Core Questions

- What does it mean to read Proust as an apprenticeship in signs?
- Is memory awakened, or is it constructed under the pressure of signs?
- How does narrative time shape the subject?
- Why is self-deception an epistemological problem?

### Toward Proust

Begin with signs as pressures rather than messages. Return to Proust through scenes where memory, desire, and misrecognition force the subject to read before understanding.

### Website Outputs

- [[Reading-Madeleine|Madeleine]]
- A short note on signs and involuntary memory.
- A map from peripheral theory back to a Proustian scene.

## 2. Music, Ineffability, and Form

### Peripheral Sources

- Jean-Jacques Nattiez, Proust as Musician
- Vladimir Jankélévitch, Music and the Ineffable
- Arthur Schopenhauer, The World as Will and Representation
- Adorno
- Schoenberg

### Core Questions

- Why is music not decoration but an apparatus of artistic truth?
- Is the ineffable a failure of language, or another form of knowledge?
- How does music turn sensory experience into structure?
- How does Vinteuil's little phrase connect love, memory, and self-deception?

### Toward Proust

Enter through music as a form that organizes feeling before it becomes concept. Return to Vinteuil as a scene where love, time, and artistic form become inseparable.

- A note on musical form as a model for narrative time.

### Core Questions

- Does seeing mean understanding?
- Why does Elstir paint perception rather than objects?
- Why does Vermeer's yellow wall become an aesthetic event before death?
- Is attention a personal faculty or a modern regime?

### Toward Proust

Begin with the instability of seeing. Return to Proust through image-events where attention changes what the eye can receive.

## 4. Space, Sensory History, and Social Time

### Peripheral Sources

- Alain Corbin, The Foul and the Fragrant

- Fernand Braudel, On History

### Core Questions

- How does a room store time?
- Is the salon a background or a machine for producing subjects?

### Toward Proust

Enter through rooms, voices, weather, smell, and ritual. Return to Proust through spaces that record social time and train perception.
