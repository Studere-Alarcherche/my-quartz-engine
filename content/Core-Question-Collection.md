# Core Question Collection

## Unit 1: Method and Entry

1. Why is Proust unsuitable for a linear syllabus?
2. What does it mean to read Proust as an apprenticeship in signs?
3. Why should this course be a map rather than a directory?
4. How can one enter Proust from the periphery?

## Unit 2: Text, Time, and Signs

1. Is memory awakened, or is it constructed under the pressure of signs?
2. How does the text produce truth?
3. How does narrative time shape the subject?
4. How does Proust turn time into space?
5. Why is self-deception an epistemological problem?

## Unit 3: Music, Ineffability, and Form

1. Why is music not decoration but an apparatus of artistic truth?
2. Is the ineffable a failure of language, or another form of knowledge?
3. How does music turn sensory experience into structure?
4. How does Vinteuil's little phrase connect love, memory, and self-deception?
5. Can the novel organize time like music?

## Unit 4: Visuality, Attention, and Image

1. Does seeing mean understanding?
2. Why does Elstir paint perception rather than objects?
3. Why does Vermeer's yellow wall become an aesthetic event before death?
4. Is attention a personal faculty or a modern regime?
5. How does photography transform identity, desire, and memory?

## Unit 5: Space, Sensory History, and Social Time

1. How does a room store time?
2. Is the salon a background or a machine for producing subjects?
3. How can sound become historical evidence?
4. How does smell connect private memory with social order?
5. How do weather, season, and atmosphere participate in narrative?

## Unit 6: Writing and Course-Making

1. How can reading become a course page?
2. How is a Reading Page different from an essay?
3. What kind of question deserves to enter the website?
4. How does form reduce the loss of meaning in transmission?
5. How can human-AI coupling expand, compress, and stabilize the system?
