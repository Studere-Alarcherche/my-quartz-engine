# Reading Page｜Vermeer Yellow Wall

## Entrance Question

Why does Vermeer's yellow wall become an aesthetic event before death?

## Peripheral Sources

- Mieke Bal, The Mottled Screen
- Jonathan Crary, Suspensions of Perception
- Maurice Merleau-Ponty, Eye and Mind
- Eric Karpeles, Paintings in Proust

## Toward Proust

The yellow wall turns looking into an event of belated attention. It asks whether an image can reveal form only when the viewer is forced to confront the limits of life and perception.

## Key Scene

Bergotte sees the small patch of yellow wall in Vermeer and experiences it as a standard of artistic necessity.

## Reading Notes

- Detail can reorganize the whole image.
- Attention is historical, bodily, and fragile.
- Aesthetic truth appears as a demand on form.

## Possible Output

A reading note on pictorial detail, mortality, and attention.
