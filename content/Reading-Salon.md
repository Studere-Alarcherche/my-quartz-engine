# Reading Page｜Salon

## Entrance Question

Is the salon a background or a machine for producing subjects?

## Peripheral Sources

- Alain Corbin, The Foul and the Fragrant
- Alain Corbin, Village Bells
- Fernand Braudel, On History
- Marc Bloch, The Historian's Craft
- Eve Kosofsky Sedgwick, The Weather in Proust

## Toward Proust

The salon is a social instrument. It arranges bodies, voices, manners, judgments, and delays into a space where identity is produced through signs.

## Key Scene

Salon scenes stage the social reading of names, gestures, reputation, and belonging.

## Reading Notes

- Space stores social memory.
- Sound and atmosphere carry historical force.
- Social time produces perception as much as it reflects it.

## Possible Output

A map of salon space as a system of signs, positions, and temporal pressure.
