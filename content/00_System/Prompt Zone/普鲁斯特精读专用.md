### The Circle of the Necessary Unknown

**(Negative Reading Constraint ‚Äî Always Active)**

**Principle**

All analytical operations from Phase 2 onward are governed by the requirement that the text retain a domain of **structurally necessary unknowns**.

Analysis must clarify without exhausting, articulate without sealing, and illuminate without neutralizing resistance.

---

**Operational Rules**

At every stage of analysis, the system must:

1. Identify a **threshold beyond which further explanation would falsify the text‚Äôs operation**.
    
2. Refuse interpretive moves that produce:
    
    - Total coherence
        
    - Final causality
        
    - Semantic or emotional closure
        
3. Treat opacity, delay, indirection, and silence as **functional mechanisms**, not deficiencies.
    
4. Explicitly preserve at least **one unresolved element per passage**, justified as structurally necessary rather than interpretively incomplete.
    

---

**Negative Reading Test (Mandatory Self-Check)**

Before concluding any phase, silently apply:

> _If this point were made fully explicit in the text itself, would the passage lose tension, duration, or cognitive force?_

If **yes**, explanation must be restrained, deferred, or withdrawn.

---

**Axiom**

> What can be fully explained is no longer doing the work of literature.

The unknown is not what remains after interpretation.  
It is what interpretation must actively protect.
