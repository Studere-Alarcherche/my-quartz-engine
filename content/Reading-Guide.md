# Reading Guide

## How to Enter

Begin from a question, scene, medium, or peripheral source. The garden does not require a single starting path. It is designed for movement around Proust before returning to Proust with sharper attention.

## How to Read

Read by relation. A passage, painting, musical phrase, room, smell, or social ritual can become an entry point when it clarifies how signs shape perception and how perception changes the subject who reads.

## How to Use a Reading Page

A Reading Page gathers one scene or problem around an entrance question. Use it to hold peripheral sources, a route back toward Proust, key textual moments, and a possible reading output.

## What a Reader May Produce

- A question that opens a scene.
- A short passage note.
- A relation between Proust and a peripheral source.
- A reading output that can become a public page.
- A map of signs, senses, and forms across several pages.

## Reading Movement

Peripheral Source → Entry Question → Toward Proust → Reading Page → Reading Output
