# Literature Importance Ranking V0.1

This ranking keeps Proust at the center while sorting supporting literature by use, timing, and evidence value.

## Level 1｜Core Field

- Marcel Proust, À la recherche du temps perdu

Use:
The central field of reading, evidence, scenes, problems, and aesthetic truth.

Why it matters:
Every method lens, bridge text, and expansion node must return to Proust rather than replacing him as the center.

When to read:
Continuously, scene by scene, with attention to signs, sensory experience, time, misrecognition, and form.

NotebookLM first?
Yes. This is the primary evidence base and should enter NotebookLM before any secondary structure becomes public.

## Level 2｜Primary Method Lenses

- Gilles Deleuze, Proust and Signs
- Gérard Genette, Narrative Discourse
- Joshua Landy, Philosophy as Fiction
- Jean-Jacques Nattiez, Proust as Musician
- Vladimir Jankélévitch, Music and the Ineffable
- Mieke Bal, The Mottled Screen
- Jonathan Crary, Suspensions of Perception
- Alain Corbin, The Foul and the Fragrant
- Alain Corbin, Village Bells

Use:
Provide the first working lenses for signs, narrative time, philosophical fiction, music, visual attention, and sensory history.

Why it matters:
These sources turn peripheral entry points into precise routes back toward Proust.

When to read:
After identifying a Proustian scene or question, then again when shaping verified question blocks.

NotebookLM first?
Yes, after Proust. These sources should be uploaded early as method anchors for evidence checking.

## Level 3｜Disciplinary Anchors

- Maurice Merleau-Ponty, Eye and Mind
- T. J. Clark, The Painting of Modern Life
- Arthur Schopenhauer, The World as Will and Representation
- Fernand Braudel, On History
- Marc Bloch, The Historian's Craft
- Lucien Febvre, A New Kind of History
- Jacques Le Goff, History and Memory
- The Cambridge Companion to Proust
- Proust and the Arts
- Proust and the Visual

Use:
Stabilize claims across perception, visual modernity, music, historical method, memory, and Proust studies.

Why it matters:
They prevent the reading garden from becoming only associative by giving each axis disciplinary weight.

When to read:
When a reading path needs stronger conceptual support or when a public page risks overextension.

NotebookLM first?
Selective yes. Upload by packet need, especially for visuality, music, and historical evidence questions.

## Level 4｜Orientation / Bridge Texts

- Adam Watt, The Cambridge Introduction to Marcel Proust
- Roger Shattuck, Proust's Way
- David Ellison, A Reader's Guide to Proust's In Search of Lost Time
- Eric Karpeles, Paintings in Proust
- Eve Kosofsky Sedgwick, The Weather in Proust

Use:
Offer orientation, scene access, and bridge language between close reading and public-facing explanation.

Why it matters:
These texts help visitors enter Proust without flattening the project into summary.

When to read:
Before drafting public-facing pages or when a packet needs a clean bridge from question to passage.

NotebookLM first?
Yes for bridge support, but after Proust and the primary method lenses.

## Level 5｜Expansion Nodes

- Adorno
- Schoenberg
- Rosen
- Thomas Mann, Doctor Faustus
- Warburg
- Lévi-Strauss
- Didi-Huberman
- Sara Danius, The Senses of Modernism
- Shattuck, The Banquet Years

Use:
Expand specific constellations around modernism, music, image memory, myth, sensory modernity, and artistic form.

Why it matters:
They widen the field without becoming the main architecture of the site.

When to read:
Only after a Proustian question has become stable and needs a wider comparative horizon.

NotebookLM first?
No, except for targeted packets where a verified expansion would clarify an existing Proust-centered question.

## Level 6｜Not Now / Archive

- broad biography
- full music history surveys
- full art history surveys
- broad poststructuralist expansion
- large Zotero evidence-chain work
- database-heavy website features

Use:
Hold useful but currently distracting directions outside the first public reading structure.

Why it matters:
The project needs density before scale, and public pages should not be pulled into archive management or encyclopedic coverage.

When to read:
Later, only when a specific reading page or packet requires it.

NotebookLM first?
No. These materials should wait until the core evidence packets are stable.
