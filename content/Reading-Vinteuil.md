# Reading Page｜Vinteuil

## Entrance Question

How does Vinteuil's little phrase connect love, memory, and self-deception?

## Peripheral Sources

- Jean-Jacques Nattiez, Proust as Musician
- Vladimir Jankélévitch, Music and the Ineffable
- Arthur Schopenhauer, The World as Will and Representation

## Toward Proust

Music enters the novel as form, pressure, recurrence, and revelation. The little phrase is not background ornament; it organizes desire and exposes the instability of interpretation.

## Key Scene

Swann hears Vinteuil's little phrase and binds it to love, longing, and the imagined truth of Odette.

## Reading Notes

- Music produces structure without direct paraphrase.
- Repetition changes what the phrase can mean.
- Ineffability becomes a mode of knowledge, not a dead end.

## Possible Output

A phrase-map showing how musical recurrence reshapes love and memory.
